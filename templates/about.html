Poker for Beginners

This website was designed for people who are just starting to play poker and would like to practice their skills in playing without the risk of losing money or being intimidated or harassed by other players in live online games.


New players need to learn and familiarize themselves with the different poker hands as well as knowing when to proceed to the next street (flop, turn, river) or when to fold their hands to minimize loss, while having fun.

The game is between a player (you) and the dealer (your computer). In a new game, the player is given $5,000 initial stack. Once the stack is down to $400, it is automatically replenished back to $5,000.


Pre-flop bet is $20 which is automatically taken from the player's stack. On the flop, the player decides to proceed to the turn with a $30 bet, or fold, losing only the initial $20 bet.

On the turn, the player has to bet $50 to continue to the river, or fold, losing $50 (bet on preflop $20 and flop $30). On the river, the bet is $100 to proceed to the showdown, or fold, losing $100. At showdown, the player either wins or lose the total pot of $400. Note: In case you encounter error in a page, click refresh to get back to the game.
